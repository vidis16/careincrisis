Matters of the Mind

 

Emotional Safety

At most points in time, we persevere in the direction of safety. Having a safe environment, being with people who make you feel safe.

Research shows that the autonomic nervous system mediates safety, trust, and intimacy through a subsystem called the social engagement system. Only when you are safe, the nervous system allows you to listen and empathize, and also be creative and innovative.
Emotional safety means that when you are with someone in a certain environment, you are internally relaxed. You feel that you can be open, be your authentic self, share your fears and hurtful memories, and feel comfortable sharing them. Through it all, you feel that you are being listened to, held with care and kindness. This safe space allows for deepening of intimacy.
How can you create an emotionally safe relationship?
> First, identify if you associate emotional safety with having a lot of people to talk or having a few or one person.

> Ask yourself if you listen to them actively without being judgemental. Allowing someone to express freely deepens the emotional bond. This does not mean that you have power over them or vice versa. This relationship is one where there is not dominating, rather an understanding one which is based on compassion for one another.

>When arguments take place, the focus must be on resolving them, not winning. This means that when the arguments heat up, it becomes unsafe and therefore to bring back the safety of the relationship, allow yourselves to take a break or some time out to cool down and then come back to the discussion.

>Talk about emotional safety. Discuss with the other person, what makes you feel safe/unsafe. Share how you can do things differently. And apologize where appropriate or say that you feel bad for hurting them.

>All is not so serious. Playfulness is one of the pillars of emotional safety. A relationship where you can bring out your inner child and can laugh and joke and be playful is marked emotionally safe.
In this emotionally safe relationship, you will have the courage to express what you want and maintain healthy boundaries.

Considering the way our brains are wired, naturally we become defensive when there seems to be a threat, in terms of comments or arguments. Rather than holding it in and seething with anger about it, expression of hurt will help in strengthening your relationship. For example, if your friend comments "I didn't expect you to do that." And you perceived that as a negative comment about you, state that it was hurtful. Seek to clarify.
