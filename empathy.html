Matters of the Mind

 

Empathy for All

When we put ourselves in another person’s shoes, we are often more sensitive to what that person is experiencing. However, it isn't always easy.

We have certain biases which we have developed over the years, some of them through media, while some through interactions with family and friends.

Social psychology shows us that these biases are developed to help ease the process of sifting through information and classifying them. However, as we see today, these biases are leading people to be insensitive towards others due to one's negative preconceived notions about the other.

Considering that we live in a multicultural, multifaith society, it is important that we have empathy and tolerance towards others.
What can you do? How can you be more empathetic towards those who are different from you?
Firstly, being empathetic or understanding others requires one to train their mind to not react to others based on these biases. For that one needs to understand the perspective of the other. This is possible by reading literature on different cultures, faiths, race, and understand why they have certain practices, and what is their status today. Realize the different experiences that people have because of belonging to a certain section of the society.
Listen to people. Listening helps in understanding the other better. We often listen long enough to formulate a counter argument or a reply, but not deep enough to know accurately what they are trying to convey. You may not agree with what others say, but allow them to express and listen to them.
Know your privilege (or lack of) and look at ways to advocate justice for all. This does not mean that social media is the only platform to share your opinions. Expressing your support can be by listening, contributing to organisations by working with them or financial aid. You could also sign petitions or write articles. Advocacy for justice for all can begin at home, with you talking and discussing about issues and presenting the different perspectives.
Your mental health is shaped by social and societal influence. Recognize your biases and develop empathy for all.
