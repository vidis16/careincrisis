Matters of the Mind

 

Gratitude Journal

We can find ourselves experiencing an influx of emotions during these stressful times. One can feel anxious, sad, fearful and angry as well. While it is important to welcome all of these emotions, it is also important to balance them with mechanisms to enhance our well-being.

A Gratitude journal typically entails, writing down a few things from our lives (daily/weekly) that we are thankful for. It is seen as a mechanism for grounding oneself in the present and focusing on the positive elements of our lives. 

While there is no particular way in which your personal journal should be, some cues can be helpful to start:
1. Start your day with noting down what you're grateful for.
2. Best part of your day.
3.People in your life that you are thankful for.
4. Ending your day with all that you are grateful for.

You can customise your journal as you like, you can have a morning journal or a journal for your challenges and learning's or one for important people in your life. 

For kids an innovative approach can be used where they could create art work (drawings, origami) to express what they are grateful for or simply discuss it with their parents before bed.

Couples can work on a collaborative journal where they could discuss what makes them happy and what challenges they face so as to share feelings with one another and take on a collaborative approach to problem solving.

So pick up a pen, paper, colours or write a song to vocalise your gratitude and share it with your family and friends to spread the joy!
