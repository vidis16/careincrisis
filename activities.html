to understand yourself a little better!

Doodling can help!

But how?

Many people doodle when they are in class or in meetings or when their mind seems to wander. Doodling is generally a drawing made while a person’s mind is otherwise occupied.

For some, doodling is a way to focus on tasks. Some use it to relieve stress. Some retain information better if they doodle while listening to it.
Through experiences of people, it can be said that the repetitive and rhythmic motion used in doodling helps people. Doodles may have similarities or have repeated patterns or designs, however every doodle is unique. 

Doodling can also be used as a mindfulness technique. This means that the person becomes aware of themselves in the present moment, without letting their mind wander. It is a way of focusing consciously and on purpose without making judgements.

To doodle mindfully, one needs to be fully engaged in the doodling activity. Mindful doodling, like other art based therapy practices can help people get in touch with their emotions, and express them through this medium. Often we find it difficult to talk about or describe how we feel; words seem to not capture the essence of what we really want to express. In that case, doodling can be useful. Also when the emotions are confronting, or too complicated for words, doodling can help you process the emotions. The idea is not to replace words, rather go beyond it.

Doodling of this nature, can be done by anyone. It does not require one to have an artistic ability, rather it is a way for us to express ourselves. There is no one way to doodle, it is flexible and unique to you.
All you really need, is something to doodle with and something to doodle on!

Here’s a short doodling exercise-
What do you need?
A paper and a pen/pencil or any other instrument
●Take a deep breath in, and breathe out.
●Take a moment to close your eyes and notice how you feel in this moment. Take a couple of deep breaths while you notice how you feel.
●When you are ready, open your eyes.
●Start doodling.
Notice the way the instrument moves against the paper. How are you holding the instrument? How much pressure are you using? What colour is your doodle? How is your arm moving? How is your posture? Notice how you are in the moment, how you feel as you doodle.
●Are you getting distracted? If so, that is fine. Notice it and bring your focus back to your task.
●Doodle as much as you like, and take your time.
●When you feel you are done, pause. In that moment, if a feeling or thought comes to mind note it down on your page.
●At this point to complete your doodle, you can add one more thing to it. It can be anything of your choice.
