Matters of the Mind

 

Exercise and Mental Health

Exercise can promote psychological well-being as well as improve quality of life.

It helps in:

• Improved mood
• Reduced stress as well as an improved ability to cope with stress
• Improved self-esteem
• Pride in physical accomplishments • Increased satisfaction with oneself
• Improved body image
• Increased feelings of energy
• Improved in confidence in your physical abilities • Decreased symptoms associated with depression


How much exercise is needed to produce those effects?
A brief walk at low intensity can improve mood and increase energy. As little as 10 minutes of exercise can have a positive effect.
For long-term benefits, you should exercise 3 times a week for 30 minutes per session at a moderate intensity.

Exercising longer than 10 weeks work best for reducing symptoms of depression.


Ways to do physical activity at home
• Walking
• Spot jogging
• Skipping
• Dancing
• Stair stepping
• Cleaning - mopping house
• Work out - water bottle weight lifting
• High Knees
• Jumping jacks


Your physical health is as important as your mental health.
