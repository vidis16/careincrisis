Matters of the Mind

 

Story Telling for Healing

Stories create awe and wonder in our minds. Stories are a way of communicating culture. It bonds different people together. Stories can help people understand each other. It influences the emotional lives of people by making them empathic towards the story teller.

Storytelling can be of various types: creating a fantasy, narrating a story from one’s past, creating a fictional story, be it with characters that are human or animals, like Jataka Tales or Panchtantra tales. One can narrate the story of one’s own life in the form of a story book.

Research has shown that storytelling can decrease stress, increase coping and empathy.

It can be useful to engage people of different age groups.

Creativity of children can be enhanced through this strategy.

With elderly, narrating stories is a way of reminiscing their life.

Parents can engage in sharing stories of their lives with children and improve interpersonal relationship.


My Life story is a technique for recognising one's own thoughts and beliefs about themselves and recognise patterns in their lives. It helps one recognise that the past has shaped them, but it does not define them.
It helps people understand that they define their own life story.
